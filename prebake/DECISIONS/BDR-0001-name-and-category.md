# BDR-0001 — Name "Sprout" and category "plant-care sidekick"

**Status:** accepted
**Date:** 2026-06-03
**Decision owner:** Fernando (demo)

## Context

We have one idea (`core/idea.md`) and three research briefs (market, competitor,
audience). The market is crowded with capable "tracker"/"manager" apps you must
remember to open. The recurring user pain is effort and silence, not lack of
features. We need a name and a category before we can write a line of copy.

## Decision

- **Name: Sprout.** Short, warm, plant-native, easy to say and spell.
- **Category: plant-care sidekick.** A sidekick works *for* you and reaches out —
  it frames Sprout as proactive and low-effort, the opposite of "tracker."

## Alternatives considered

- **"PlantPal" / "LeafLog"** — generic; "Log" implies user labor (the thing we're
  removing).
- **Category "smart plant tracker"** — accurate but sits us *inside* the crowded
  destination-app frame instead of contrasting with it.

## Consequences

- All copy must use *sidekick / nudge / care day* (see `product-vocabulary.md`).
- Positioning leads with "comes to you," not "comprehensive."
- The first CTA is a **waitlist** — we're validating the proactive promise, not
  shipping features yet.
