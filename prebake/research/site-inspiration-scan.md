# Site-inspiration scan — great founder/personal sites

**Written by:** `site-scan` agent · **Source:** `core/idea.md`
**Status:** working draft (patterns, not prescriptions)

## What the best founder sites have in common

- **One clear sentence up top.** Who you are + what you're building, before any scrolling.
- **A single primary action.** Usually "get in touch" / "book time" — not five competing CTAs.
- **Proof over adjectives.** Concrete work shown, not "visionary leader."
- **Personality, restrained.** A voice, a point of view — but fast-loading and uncluttered.
- **A quiet door to more.** Links to writing, talks, the company — without crowding the hero.

## Patterns worth stealing

- Hero = name + role + one belief + one button. (Ours: "memory that belongs to you" + *Book time with me*.)
- A short, honest "About" in first person.
- "Work" as a few cards, newest/most-important first.
- A private back office is unusual for a personal site — and a great differentiator: the site *works for you*, not just at visitors.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Walls of buzzwords; auto-playing media; a resume dump; five social icons fighting the CTA.

## Implication

Lead with the belief and the booking CTA. Keep the public surface to one page +
`/book`. Put the CRM behind a quiet `/login`. Let the *booking → CRM* loop be the
thing that feels magic.
