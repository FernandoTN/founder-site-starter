# Market scan — plant-care apps

**Written by:** `market-scan` agent · **Source:** `core/idea.md`
**Status:** working draft (illustrative; figures marked as estimates)

## The shape of the market

Houseplants are a large, durable consumer category that surged during the
pandemic and settled into a steady hobby for millions of households. Plant
ownership skews younger and urban; apartment-dwellers with limited outdoor space
over-index. The recurring pain is not *buying* plants — it's *keeping them alive*.

## Why people reach for an app

- They own more plants than they can track by memory.
- Watering needs differ wildly by species, season, light, and pot size.
- The failure is silent: by the time a plant looks sick, it's often too late.
- Existing reminders (calendar, sticky notes) are generic and ignored.

## Where the money is

- **Freemium subscriptions** dominate (a free tier to hook, a paid tier for
  unlimited plants / advanced features). Typical paid tiers sit in the
  low-single-digit dollars per month. *(Estimate — confirm before quoting.)*
- Secondary: affiliate sales of supplies (soil, pots, fertilizer), partnerships
  with nurseries.

## Implication for Sprout

The wedge is **proactive, personalized nudges** — "text me *before* it's a
problem" — not another static care encyclopedia. A waitlist is the right first
test: it costs nothing to build and tells us if the proactive angle resonates.
