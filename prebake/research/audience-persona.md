# Audience persona — "Plant-hopeful Priya"

**Written by:** `audience-persona` agent · **Source:** `core/idea.md`
**Status:** working draft

## Who she is

Priya, 31, lives in a city apartment, works a demanding job. She loves how plants
make her space feel alive and has bought ~8 over two years. She has killed ~5 of
them — usually by forgetting to water, occasionally by drowning them out of guilt.

## What she feels

- **Guilt** when a plant dies ("I can keep a job but not a pothos").
- **Low-grade anxiety** — she's never sure if today is a watering day.
- **No appetite for a chore.** She will not log data daily. She *will* answer a text.

## What she wants

- To be told **what to do, when, in one line** — no dashboards.
- To feel like a competent plant person without studying horticulture.
- Proof it works before she pays anything → a **waitlist** is a low-commitment yes.

## How Sprout wins her

A text the day before a plant needs water: *"Your fiddle-leaf fig wants a drink
tomorrow 🌿"* — one tap to mark done. Zero app-opening required. The brand voice
should feel like a **calm, capable friend**, never a nagging tool.

## Message that lands

> "Keep your plants alive without thinking about it."
