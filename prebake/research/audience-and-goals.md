# Audience & goals — who visits, what they want

**Written by:** `audience` agent · **Source:** `core/idea.md`
**Status:** working draft

## Who lands on a founder's site

| Visitor | What they want | What converts them |
|---|---|---|
| **Recruiter / collaborator** | Is this person real and reachable? | Clear role + an easy way to book time |
| **Investor** | What are they building, and how do they think? | The belief line + the Memori card |
| **Press / event organizer** | A fast, accurate bio + contact | True, sourced "About" + booking |
| **Peer / student** | Learn from them; maybe connect | Writing/talks + low-friction booking |

## The one goal

Turn a warm visitor into a **booked conversation** — and capture them in the CRM so
the relationship doesn't evaporate. Everything else is secondary.

## Design implications

- The **Book** button is the primary CTA on every public surface.
- The booking form asks the minimum (name, email, slot, optional note).
- Each booking auto-creates a **contact** — the Rolodex builds itself.
- Tone: credible and warm, never salesy. A personal site is a handshake.
