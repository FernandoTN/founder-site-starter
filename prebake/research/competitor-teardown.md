# Competitor teardown — Planta & Greg

**Written by:** `competitor-teardown` agent · **Source:** `core/idea.md`
**Status:** working draft (positioning observations, not pricing quotes)

## Planta

- **Positioning:** the polished, comprehensive plant-care companion. Big species
  database, light-meter, care schedules, beautiful UI.
- **Strengths:** breadth, design, brand trust.
- **Gaps:** can feel like *work* — lots of setup and logging. The burden is on
  the user to open the app.

## Greg

- **Positioning:** smart, friendly, a bit playful; personalized watering plans
  that adapt to weather and conditions.
- **Strengths:** personalization, approachable tone, community.
- **Gaps:** still fundamentally an app you must *remember to open*.

## The shared blind spot → Sprout's wedge

Both are **destinations**: value lives inside the app, and the user has to go get
it. Sprout inverts that — it **comes to you** (a text before your plant is in
trouble) and asks for almost nothing in return. "The plant-care app you don't
have to open."

## Naming/category note

"Sidekick" / "the app that texts you" frames Sprout as *proactive* and *low-effort*
— a contrast to "tracker" or "manager," which imply user labor.
