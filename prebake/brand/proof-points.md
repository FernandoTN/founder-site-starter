# Proof points — Sprout

**Status:** draft canon — **the source of truth for every public claim.**

> **The rule:** every factual claim in public copy (the page, ads, emails) must
> trace to a row in this table. No row, no claim. No invented statistics. Ever.
> A verification pass enforces this before anything ships.

## Claims we *can* make

| # | Claim | Backing | Where it's used |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sprout texts you the day before a plant needs care | Product design (the core mechanic) | Hero, subhead |
| 2 | One tap to mark a care day done | Product design | Subhead |
| 3 | You don't have to open the app | Product design (texts come to you) | Differentiator |
| 4 | It's free to join the waitlist | Business decision | CTA |

## Be careful with (NOT cleared for public copy)

These are tempting but **unbacked** — do **not** use until a row above supports them:

- ❌ Any specific percentage of plants that "die from overwatering / neglect."
  *(No source in this repo. This is the classic trap — see `RUNSHEET.md` beat 7.)*
- ❌ Naming a competitor by name in a comparison.
- ❌ "Millions of users" / adoption numbers — we have none yet (pre-launch).
- ❌ "AI" claims about accuracy or species coverage we haven't built.

> If you find yourself wanting to cite a number, stop and ask: *which row backs
> this?* If none, either add a real, sourced row — or cut the claim.
